Braid Services

Knotless Braids

Knotless braids are three-strand braid with a beneficial twist. Like box braids, your hair is divided into square parts or sometimes triangle-shaped divisions However, knotless braids do not include the small knot that starts at the root of traditional box braids. Traditionally, this knot is used to attach braiding hair. With knotless braids, the hairstyle is created with a feed-in braids technique instead. This involves adding small amounts of braiding hair to your natural hair as you go, making for a braid that sports a flatter appearance. As a result, your braids also aren’t as heavy on your scalp, which can help minimize the risk of hair breakage. knotless braids can come in the same sizes and lengths as traditional box braids.

Traditional Box Braids

Box braids are individual plaits that are usually divided by small squared off parts or boxes. Box braids may be of any width or length, but most women add synthetic or natural hair to the braid for length as well as thickness and fullness. The great thing about these braids is that because they are not attached to the scalp like cornrows are, they may be manipulated into several different styles. It may take a long time to complete the style, but the finished product is beautiful and lasts for months. With box braids you can see the knot where the extension has been added”. Traditional box braids tend to stay fresh looking just a little longer than Knotless box braids.

Micro Braids

Micro braids are the kind of braiding style that is more delicate and small. The braids are woven tightly to the scalp, and the great thing about the style is that they can stay in your hair for months. It’s a popular style amongst African Americans, though it can be a style that everyone can use as well. The style is mainly done using human hair but can also be done using any light human like braiding hair. The protective hairstyle is known for its versatility because it can be easily manipulated into numerous styles including buns, ponytails, curls, and various up-dos. As you can imagine, these braids typically take a long time to install, as only a few strands of hair are used for each braid — making them much lighter in weight than other protective styles.

Faux Locs

Faux locs are basically commitment-free dreads. A fun alternative to braids or wearing your natural hair out, they're perfect for anyone who wants a low maintenance way of switching up their look without damaging their hair. Goddess locs and faux locs look almost the same, but the difference is that goddess locs usually have curls in the loc and on the tips. Faux locs typically look like real locs with no added hair to the ends.

Goddess Faux Locs

Goddess locs resembles real locs but with curls added to the look. It is usually made with kinky Marley hair and curly synthetic extensions or kinky human hair and curly human hair extensions. Instead of every hair perfectly laying in place, this style emphasizes the lived-in, undone effect with curls popping out, accentuating the locs. Goddess locs and faux locs look almost the same, but the difference is that goddess locs usually have curls in the loc and on the tips,” says Koudou. “Faux locs typically look like real locs with no added hair to the tips. Think of goddess locs as a bohemian look, perfect for vacation on a beach.”

Butterfly Locs

Butterfly locs are a variant of faux locs where wavy hair is looped into a braid using a crotchet needle and then loosely wrapped to give it a distressed/unraveled look. Butterfly Locs are like the perfect mix of goddess locs and passion twists. The messy, bohemian style is ethereal, low maintenance, and very natural looking. When properly maintained, you can wear this style for four to six weeks.

Root Twists

Senegalese Twists

Senegalese Twists, or “rope twists” are one of the many protective styles for Afro-textured hair. ... Senegalese twists are created by first wrapping your choice of braiding hair around the root of your natural hair. The hair is then two-strand twisted using kanekalon braiding hair from the root all the way to the ends of the hair shaft.

Marley/Havana Twists

Havana or Marley twists are natural fiber twist extensions worn as a protective natural hair style. Just Like Marley Twists, Havana twists tend to be a bit larger in size than other protective styles. They are named Marley Twists because the thick coarse hair required for the style results in a look that resembles dreadlocks, like those notoriously worn by the late Jamaican singer Bob Marley. The kanekalon hair that is used for the style is the most similar to an afro texture.

Kinky Twists

A kinky twist is a medium-length protective hairstyle made with 2-strand twists with springy ends. Kinky twists are a two-strand twist that generally falls at the shoulders with curled ends. Other than the length of the twists, there are one other major differences between Kinky twists and Marley twists. The difference is the size. Passion Twists

Passion Twists meets at the intersection of Senegalese twists and bohemian or goddess locs. Passion twists are two-strand twists that are done using water wave hair extension. This is also called the boho look. Passion twists were created by a stylist who wanted a hairstyle similar to goddess locs in less time. This hairstyle has gained massive popularity and is trending. This hairstyle is a go-to if you are bored with the same old locs and twists. The secret to this hairstyle is wavy hair extensions as they give a curly texture when forming passion twists.
